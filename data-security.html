Data & Security

InventoryOS stores business-critical inventory, sales, receipt, label and integration data. This page explains what is stored and the protections used.

On this page Overview Stored data Protection Admin access Gmail Retention Your responsibility

1. Overview

InventoryOS is designed to store operational business data such as inventory, stock locations, sales, expenses, receipts and shipping-label files. This page summarises how those areas are handled.

2. What is stored

InventoryProducts, barcodes, quantities, locations, movement history and workspace settings.
Sales and finance recordsSales, refunds, expenses, receipt uploads, corrections, revenue and profit calculations.
Uploaded filesReceipts, shipping labels, PDFs, image files and generated/cropped label outputs.
IntegrationsGmail OAuth connection details, label import metadata, QZ printing preferences and signing requests.
BillingPlan, status, Stripe IDs and subscription events. Full card details are handled by Stripe.
Account securityPassword hashes, verification state, login timestamps, admin/suspension flags and last-seen activity.

3. Security measures

  • HTTPS is used for app and API traffic.
  • Passwords are stored as hashes rather than plain text.
  • Private uploads are intended to be protected by authenticated routes.
  • Workspace permissions limit what users can access.
  • Admin tools are restricted to authorised admin accounts.
  • QZ private keys and app secrets should be stored outside public folders and not committed to source control.

4. Admin and support access

Authorised InventoryOS admins may access account, workspace, plan, billing-status and usage information to provide support, investigate bugs, manage beta testers, apply complimentary plan overrides, handle abuse and keep the service secure.

5. Gmail and shipping labels

Gmail connection is optional. When connected, InventoryOS uses Google OAuth to access shipping-label emails and attachments for label import. Users can disconnect Gmail, and reconnecting may be required if permissions or secrets change.

6. Storage and retention

Operational records and uploaded files are kept while your account is active and for a reasonable period afterwards for recovery, audit, security, legal, support or dispute reasons. You can request deletion by contacting hello@inventoryos.co.uk.

7. Your responsibility

You are responsible for keeping your password secure, checking that your business records are accurate, only uploading files you are allowed to process, and exporting any records you need before deleting your account.
